B2B Pricing Strategies: How to Set Wholesale Prices That Sell

W
Will
Distribution Expert

Setting the right wholesale price is one of the most critical decisions for B2B brands. Price too high, and distributors look elsewhere. Price too low, and you erode margins. The sweet spot requires understanding your costs, your market, and your distributors' needs.

The Wholesale Pricing Formula

Start with your cost structure and work backwards:

  • Manufacturing Cost: $50
  • Target Margin: 40%
  • Wholesale Price: $83.33
  • Retail Markup: 2.5x
  • MSRP: $208.33

Tiered Pricing Models

Reward volume with better margins:

  • Tier 1: 1-100 units @ $83.33 (40% margin)
  • Tier 2: 101-500 units @ $78.95 (45% margin)
  • Tier 3: 501+ units @ $74.63 (50% margin)

Common Pricing Mistakes

1. Competing on Price Alone: Race to the bottom helps no one. Compete on value, service, and brand.

2. Static Pricing: Market conditions change. Review pricing quarterly.

3. Ignoring Payment Terms: Net 30 vs Net 60 affects your cash flow. Price accordingly.

Dynamic Pricing Strategies

Modern B2B brands use dynamic pricing based on:

  • Seasonal demand patterns
  • Distributor performance
  • Competitive positioning
  • Inventory levels

Conclusion

Effective B2B pricing balances profitability with distributor incentives. Start with your costs, add your margin, then test and refine based on market response.
