# SMALL COMPANIES ONLY STRATEGY

## 🚨 CORRECTION: Target Small Companies Only

**Mistake Made:** Contacted massive companies (Acne, Oatly, Fjällräven)
**Correct Approach:** Small companies <100M SEK revenue, 10-100 employees

## Target Criteria (Strict)

### Revenue
- **Maximum:** 100M SEK (~€9M)
- **Sweet spot:** 10M-50M SEK
- **Avoid:** >100M SEK (too large)

### Employees
- **Target:** 10-100 employees
- **Sweet spot:** 20-50 employees
- **Avoid:** <10 (no B2B yet) and >200 (too bureaucratic)

### Company Profile
✅ **Ideal Small Company:**
- Growing brand (hiring on LinkedIn)
- Has some B2B presence (wholesale page on website)
- Founder/CEO accessible
- 10-100 employees
- Revenue 10M-100M SEK
- Swedish-owned

❌ **Avoid:**
- Public companies (Acne Studios, Odd Molly)
- Global giants (Oatly, Fjällräven)
- Subsidiaries of large groups
- >200 employees
- No B2B presence

## Why Small Companies?

**Advantages:**
- ✅ CEO makes decisions directly
- ✅ Faster sales cycle
- ✅ More price-sensitive (value our solution)
- ✅ Growing = needs systems
- ✅ Personal relationship possible
- ✅ Less competition

**Big Company Problems:**
- ❌ Multiple decision makers
- ❌ Long sales cycles (6-12 months)
- ❌ Procurement departments
- ❌ Already have systems
- ❌ Too bureaucratic

## How to Verify Size

### 1. LinkedIn
- Check "About" section for employee count
- Look at hiring activity
- Check company size category

### 2. Swedish Business Registry (Allabolag.se)
- Search company name
- View annual revenue
- Check number of employees
- Free basic info

### 3. Website Clues
- Small team page (5-20 people shown)
- No "Investor Relations"
- Personal founder story
- No global office listings

## Red Flags (Stop & Skip)

If you see these, SKIP the company:
- Listed on stock exchange
- >200 employees on LinkedIn
- "Global headquarters" in multiple countries
- Owned by large corporation (check ownership)
- Revenue >100M SEK on Allabolag
- No team page (too small / solopreneur)
