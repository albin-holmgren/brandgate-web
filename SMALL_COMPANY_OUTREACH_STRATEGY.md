# Outreach Strategy Update: Smaller Companies Focus

## 🎯 STRATEGY SHIFT

**From:** Large enterprises (Happy Socks, NetOnNet, etc.)  
**To:** Growing D2C brands (10-100 employees)  
**Why:** Better fit for early-stage product

---

## ✅ WHY THIS IS SMARTER

### Problems with Large Companies:
- ❌ 6-12 month sales cycles
- ❌ Complex stakeholder management (10+ people)
- ❌ Risk-averse (won't try new tools)
- ❌ Procurement departments
- ❌ Expect enterprise features you don't have yet
- ❌ High expectations, hard to please

### Benefits of Small Companies:
- ✅ Decisions in days/weeks
- ✅ Talk to the CEO/founder directly
- ✅ Willing to try new solutions
- ✅ No procurement bureaucracy
- ✅ Pain is urgent (they feel it daily)
- ✅ Easier to delight
- ✅ Better word-of-mouth
- ✅ Price point works for them

---

## 🏢 NEW TARGET PROFILE

**Size:** 10-100 employees (sweet spot: 15-50)  
**Revenue:** 10M-100M SEK  
**Growth:** 20%+ YoY (expanding)  
**Stage:** D2C → Wholesale transition  
**Digital:** E-commerce first, tech-savvy  
**Pain:** Spreadsheets breaking, manual retailer management

## 💡 PRO TIPS

1. **Lead with pain, not product**
   - "Tired of Excel?" not "We have a platform"

2. **Be conversational**
   - "Quick question" not "I'd like to discuss"

3. **Low friction CTA**
   - "15-min chat" not "Strategic assessment"

4. **Founder-to-founder tone**
   - "I bet you're dealing with..." not "Our solution enables..."

5. **Speed over features**
   - "Setup in 30 min" not "Enterprise-grade functionality"

6. **No social proof needed**
   - They don't expect testimonials from early products

7. **Direct access**
   - Email the CEO, not the procurement team
